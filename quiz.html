Size of the dog

First let's start off with the size of the dog. Not only does this give us an idea on how big the collar should be, but it also lets us know what material we should use to make the collar.

Under 30 lbs

Your dog is fairly easy to carry around without a collar and you don't want the collar to be too strong in case it hurts the dog.

30 - 50 lbs

Your dog is kinda hefty and you need a durable material that can move the dog around with ease.

Over 50 lbs

Your dog's a behemoth and it's a good idea to get the sturdiest material possible.

Breed

We don't necessarily want the exact breed of the dog, but we want a general idea on its properties.

Pudgy

Your dog is short and stout. Think pugs, weinerdogs, and corgis.

Average

Pretty normal in terms of makeup. Think golden retriever and pitbulls.

Long and Skinny

The dog might be big, but is very skinny. Think greyhounds.

ACTIVITY

Every dog is different and every dog uses their collars differently; there is no need to put an athletic collar on a couch potato and vice versa.

Lazy

Your little buddy likes to lounge around and doesn't really go out much save for the occasional walk.

Avg

As active as a regular dog. Nothing too special about this.

Active

Your dog cannot stay still for a second! It's always playing with you, a toy, or something else within vicinity.

SWAG

Put simply; how much swag does your dog have?

None

Your dog is a very plain jane type of dog. Isn't really flashy.

Very Little

There's something there, but not anything to brag about.

Swagaliscious!

Boy oh boy, is that Kanye West? Nah, that's just your dog.
